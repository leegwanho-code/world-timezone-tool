WorldClock

World Timezone Comparison Tool

How to Use

  1. Search for a city — Type any city name or country in the search box above. Results appear instantly as you type.
  2. Compare times side by side — Up to 6 city clocks are displayed simultaneously. Each card shows the current local time, date, UTC offset, and whether DST is active.
  3. Plan a meeting — Drag the Meeting Planner slider to find an overlap that works for all your cities. The slider updates all clocks at once so you can spot conflicts instantly.
  4. Remove a city — Click the × button on any card to remove it. Your city list is saved automatically for your next visit.
  5. Switch dark / light mode — Use the toggle in the top-right corner. Your preference is remembered between sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this tool free to use?
Yes. WorldClock is completely free, requires no sign-up, and has no usage limits.
Does it handle Daylight Saving Time (DST) automatically?
Yes. DST is detected automatically using your browser's built-in Intl API, so times are always accurate regardless of the time of year. Cities currently observing DST show a red DST badge.
How many cities can I compare at once?
Up to 6 cities can be displayed simultaneously. You can remove any city and add a different one at any time.
Are my cities saved between visits?
Yes. Your selected cities and display theme are stored in your browser's local storage. No data is sent to any server.
What is the Meeting Planner?
The Meeting Planner is a slider that lets you set a hypothetical time in your reference city and instantly see what time it would be in all other cities. Drag it left or right to scan through the day and find a time that works for everyone.
Does it work offline?
Yes. WorldClock performs all calculations locally in your browser using the Intl.DateTimeFormat API. No internet connection is needed after the page loads.
Which cities are supported?
Over 70 major cities across all continents are included, covering every major time zone. Search by city name or country to find the one you need.

Times update every second. DST is detected automatically using browser APIs.
